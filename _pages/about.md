I am an incoming Master's student majoring in Brain Science at National Yang Ming Chiao Tung University (NYCU), supervised by Prof. Jeng-Ren Duann. I previously graduated with distinction with a Bachelor of Education from the Department of Kinesiology at National Tsing Hua University (NTHU), where I worked under the supervision of Prof. Feng-Tzu Chen.

My core research interests lie in **Cognitive Neuroscience**, with a specific focus on utilizing **fMRI** and **EEG** techniques to understand the underlying mechanisms of the human brain. A significant portion of my research explores how physical activity modulates cognitive functions. Recently, I conducted a rigorously standardized study (n=45) funded by the NSTC, investigating the effects of low-intensity resistance exercise—specifically focusing on slow movement and tonic force generation—on executive function and cognitive flexibility in older adults. 

I have actively participated in designing research methodologies, executing experiments, and performing data analysis across multiple projects, including studies on metacognition in middle-aged adults and executive function in adolescents with hearing impairments. My work has been recognized internationally, recently earning the Best Oral Presenter Award at the 12th International Seminar of Sport and Exercise Psychology in Malaysia.

### **Research Interests**
* Cognitive Neuroscience
* Neuroimaging (fMRI, EEG)
* Exercise Psychology & Aging
